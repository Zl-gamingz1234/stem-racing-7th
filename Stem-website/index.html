What is STEM?

STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics. STEM education helps students learn concepts by doing activities, experiments, and real-life problem solving instead of only reading from textbooks.

What is STEM Racing India?

STEM Racing India is an educational program that teaches students scientific concepts through exciting learning activities. It helps students understand how science is applied in the real world using simple experiments and observations.

Science Concepts Explained

1. Force

Force is a push or a pull that can change the shape, direction, or speed of an object. Without force, objects cannot move.

Examples: pushing a door, kicking a football, pulling a drawer.

2. Motion

Motion means the movement of an object from one place to another. An object is said to be in motion when its position changes with time.

Examples: moving bus, flying bird, rolling ball.

3. Speed

Speed tells us how fast an object is moving. It is the distance travelled by an object in a given amount of time.

Formula: Speed = Distance รท Time

4. Time

Time is the duration taken by an object to complete its motion. In science experiments, time is measured using a clock or a stopwatch.

5. Friction

Friction is a force that opposes motion when two surfaces rub against each other. It slows down moving objects.

Friction is useful because it helps us walk without slipping, but too much friction can reduce speed.

6. Air Resistance

Air resistance is a type of friction caused by air. Objects moving through the air experience air resistance.

Smooth shapes experience less air resistance and move more easily.

7. Gravity

Gravity is the force that pulls objects towards the Earth. It is why objects fall when dropped.

8. Aerodynamics

Aerodynamics is the study of how air flows around an object when it moves. Whenever an object moves through air, the air applies a force on it. This force is known as air resistance or drag.

Air resistance acts in the opposite direction of motion and tries to slow the object down. Faster moving objects experience more air resistance, while slower objects experience less air resistance.

Effect of Shape on Aerodynamics

The shape of an object plays an important role in aerodynamics. Objects with flat or rough surfaces face more air resistance. Objects with smooth and curved surfaces allow air to flow easily around them.

Streamlined Shape

A streamlined shape is a smooth shape that helps reduce air resistance. This shape allows air to move smoothly around the object, helping it move faster and more efficiently.

Examples of streamlined shapes include aeroplanes, birds, fish and fast-moving cars.

Simple Experiment

If a flat sheet of paper and a crumpled paper ball are dropped at the same time, the paper ball reaches the ground first. This happens because the paper ball has better aerodynamics and experiences less air resistance.

Uses of Aerodynamics in Real Life

  • Helps vehicles move faster
  • Reduces air resistance and drag
  • Saves fuel and energy
  • Improves performance and safety
  • Used in aeroplanes, cars and sports equipment

Real Life Applications of Science

  • Force helps us push, pull and lift objects
  • Friction allows vehicles to stop using brakes
  • Speed helps compare fast and slow vehicles
  • Air resistance is used in parachutes
  • Gravity keeps us on the ground
